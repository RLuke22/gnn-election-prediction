General information about the 2020 US Election

Election date: November 3rd, 2020

How is the winner decided?

Both candidates (Joe Biden and Donald Trump) compete to win 
electoral college votes. Each state gets a certain number of 
electoral college votes mostly based on population. There are
a total of 538 electoral college votes up for grabs, so the 
winner is the candidate that wins 270 or more votes.

All but two states have a winner-takes-all rule, so whichever 
candidate wins the highest number of votes is awarded all of 
the state's electoral college votes.

What are battleground (swing) states?

A state which, either historically or based on polling data, could
lean either way is called a battleground state.
